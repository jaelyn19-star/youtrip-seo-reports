⏱ 20 min ▾

Categorise the non-indexed URLs
Monday 13 April — Sunday 19 April
⏱ ~1.5–2 hrs ▾
Goal: Sort non-indexed URLs into buckets and flag anything that shouldn't be excluded. This is the heavy lifting for April — give yourself a proper block of time.

  • Go through the URLs and assign one of these categories: Blog or article → Blog · Product/feature page → Product · Legal/T&C/privacy → Legal · Old promo/campaign → Promo · Country/destination guide → Destination · Off-brand (fitness, gaming, COVID) → Off-brand · Something looks important but you're not sure → Needs review

W4
CTR quick win: SG Public Holidays title + meta rewrite
Monday 20 April — Wednesday 30 April
⏱ 45 min ▾
Goal: Rewrite the title + meta description for the Singapore Public Holidays page. It gets ~351,000 impressions/month but only 0.32% CTR — even a small rewrite can unlock hundreds of extra free clicks per month.
  • WHERE Go to GSC → Performance → Search results → filter pages containing holiday. Note down: current impressions, CTR, and average position.
  • Open the live page on the website → copy the existing title and meta description.
  • Write a new title (max 60 characters): Include Singapore Public Holidays 2026 + a hook. Example: "Singapore Public Holidays 2026 — Full List & Dates"
  • Write a new meta description (max 155 characters): Lead with the answer immediately. Example: "Singapore has 11 public holidays in 2026. See the full list of dates — from Chinese New Year to Deepavali — and plan your long weekends."
  • Log into WordPress → open the page → find your SEO plugin: Yoast: scroll down → "Edit snippet" → update title + meta · RankMath: right panel → General tab → update title + meta
  • Save the page.
  • GSC → URL Inspection → paste the page URL → click Request Indexing. This tells Google to recrawl the page faster. Check CTR again in 4–6 weeks.
✅ Why it matters: Moving from 0.32% to 0.5% CTR on 351k impressions = ~630 extra clicks/month with zero extra content work.

W3
FAQ schema — 4 priority pages
Monday 11 May — Sunday 17 May
⏱ ~1.5 hrs ▾
What is FAQ schema? It's a small piece of code that tells Google "this page has Q&A content." When Google picks it up, your search result can show expandable questions — making your listing bigger and getting more clicks without needing to rank higher.
  • Pages to do this week: Travel Insurance Singapore · About YouTrip (Everything You Need to Know) · YouTrip Exchange Rates · Best Savings Accounts Singapore
  • For each page: Google the main keyword and look at the People Also Ask box — these are literally the questions people are typing. Pick 3–5 that appear (or should appear) in your article.
  • Open the page in WordPress → go to your SEO plugin → look for a Schema or FAQ section: Yoast Premium: Edit snippet → Schema tab → Add FAQ block · RankMath: right panel → Schema tab → Add FAQ schema · No FAQ option? Install the free plugin "Schema & Structured Data for WP" from the WordPress plugin directory
  • Add each Q&A pair. Keep answers concise — 1–3 sentences each.
  • Save each page → GSC URL Inspection → Request Indexing for each one.
  • After 1–2 weeks: search for the main keyword in Google in an incognito window and see if FAQ expandable boxes appear under your result.
✅ Done when: FAQ schema is saved on all 4 pages and each has been submitted for re-indexing.

W2
Internal linking audit — data pull
Monday 8 June — Sunday 14 June
⏱ ~1 hr ▾
What is internal linking? When one page on your site links to another page on your site. It helps Google understand which pages matter most and spreads "ranking power" to pages that need it.
  • GSC → Performance → Pages → sort by Clicks (highest first). Export the top 50 URLs.
  • Open in Google Sheets. Create columns: URL, Clicks, Impressions, Avg Position, Internal Links In.
  • For each URL, do a quick Google search: site:you.co/sg "keyword for that page" to roughly see how many other site pages mention and link to it. Or use Semrush → Site Audit → Internal Linking report → look for pages with low internal link count.
  • Flag any important pages (high impressions, decent position) with fewer than 3–4 internal links as Under-linked in a new column.
✅ Done when: top 50 pages are in a spreadsheet and under-linked ones are flagged. You'll turn this into an action brief next week.

W4
410 Gone dev ticket — off-brand page cleanup
Monday 22 June — Sunday 28 June
⏱ ~1 hr ▾
What is a 410 Gone response? It tells Google "this page is permanently removed and won't come back." This is cleaner than a 404 for pages you've deliberately deleted — it stops Google wasting time crawling dead ends.
  • Open your noindex audit spreadsheet from April → filter for Category = Off-brand (fitness, beauty, gaming, COVID pages, etc.)
  • For each URL, check in Semrush Backlink Analytics: paste the URL and look for external backlinks. If it has backlinks pointing to it → put in a "Review" list (don't 410 yet — you'd lose that link equity). If no backlinks + no GSC impressions → safe to 410.
  • Write a dev ticket (copy this template and fill in the URLs):
  • Subject: SEO Cleanup — Return 410 Gone for off-brand pages Include the list of URLs + note: "These are off-brand pages with no traffic, no backlinks, and no SEO value. Please return 410 Gone. No rush — next sprint is fine."
  • Send to dev.
✅ Why it matters: Cleaning up 40+ off-brand pages improves crawl budget — Google spends more time on your real pages instead of old fitness content.
